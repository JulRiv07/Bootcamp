Past Simple

The Simple Past tense describes actions that began and finished in the past. It's used when the time of the action is clear or implied
— for example, yesterday, last week, in 2020, two days ago, etc.
In affirmative sentences, we use the verb in the past form. Regular verbs end with -ed (worked, played, watched), while irregular verbs change completely (go → went, eat → ate, see → saw).
In negative sentences, we use did not (didn't) followed by the base form of the verb, not the past form. To make a question, we use Did, followed by the subject and the base verb.
